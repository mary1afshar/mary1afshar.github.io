About me

Hi, my name is Maryam Afshar and welcome to my personal website! I am a bubble tea enthusiastic and an aspiring software developer. Growing up I would fantasize about the immense potential of computers, a past time acquired from watching my father work as a software engineer. As I got older, my father gradually began introducing me to the world of programming, encouraging me to take various computer science courses. Ever since I’ve gravitated towards software development. Throughout my high school career, it saddened me when I noticed the lack of girls in my computer science class, hackathons and engineering tours, often being the only girl in these events. I struggled to find female friends and mentors, realizing most of my computer science teachers or tour guides were male as well. Although the absence of women in engineering is discouraging, it motivates me to make a difference. Without my father, I would not have thought of becoming an software engineer. Currently, I’m a first-year computer science student at the University of Waterloo in hopes of becoming a software engineer and that I can apply engineering knowledge to solve real-world problems including the fact that we have a shortage of women in engineering.

Projects

Over the past couple months, I have worked on a few projects at school, on my own and at hackathons.

WaterTrackers

At a recent hackathon, my team and I developed a website called WaterTrackers. The website tells you everything you need to know in case of a flood, and how at-risk you are of encountering one. Using the map, you can find your location and see how many floods occur in your area, and whether you're in danger. This data was taken from a CSV file which was prepared via a python script. You can also use it to make informed decisions about where you want to live and build communities. On our site, you can get detailed checklists to ensure you don’t forget small details. You will also find steps to take before, during and after a flood. We have useful tips to help prepare and guide you through what to do if you encounter a flood. I worked on the UI and UX of the website and exported the HTML and CSS code. My teammates worked on the Google Maps API which was embedded in the map with various flood locations on our website. 

C++ swimming class database

Within C++, I created an object-oriented database system. It is a simulated swimming class registration program where users can add, delete, modify and view all students registered in swimming classes.  Detailed steps guide users through the program by using multidimensional arrays, inheritance and pointers. 

3-D printing projects

With my father's 3-D printer, my sister and I started making very useful designs to help us in the kitchen. My family loves to cook and so we created designs to help us be more efficient in the kitchen. We recently created a cube that replaces the measuring cups and spoons. With this cube, we can measure 1/3 cup, 1/2 cup, 1 cup, 1 tsp..... We found the design on the internet but modified it using the software Solidworks.  

Python Operation game

This game was inspired by the classical operation games, I use to play in my childhood. It is created in python with the Pygame library. During a certain time limit,  users use the keyboard to collect bones without touching the border to win points. There are two levels in the game and users must pass the first level to move onto level two. Each level has its own criteria to meet and with sounds and the instruction page, the player is guided through easily. In addition, the player has the choice to exit the game at any point.
