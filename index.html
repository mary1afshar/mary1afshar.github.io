About me

Hey, nice to meet you & welcome to my personal website!

My name is Maryam Afshar, an aspiring software developer and a bubble tea enthusiastic. Growing up I would fantasize about the immense potential of computers, a past time acquired from watching my father work as a software engineer. As I got older, my father gradually began introducing me to the world of programming, encouraging me to take various computer science courses. Ever since I’ve gravitated towards software development. Throughout my high school career, it saddened me when I noticed the lack of girls in my computer science class, hackathons and university tours, often being the only girl in these events. I struggled to find female friends and mentors, realizing most of my computer science teachers or tour guides were male as well. Although the absence of women in CS and engineering is discouraging, it motivates me to make a difference. Currently, I’m a 1A Computer Science student at the University of Waterloo in hopes of becoming a software engineer and that I can apply programming knowledge to solve real-world problems. I hope to encourage and help younger girls to code in the future. I would love to get in contact with you, feel free to contact me on any of my socials!

Hobbies

SPORTS

Gymnastics and swimming, both competitive and challenging sports, have taught me the importance of giving it your best even when you see no progress. TRAVEL

After a busy semester of school, I find it a priority to take some time off to relax. Travelling allows me to learn about different cultures and re-energize; this enhances my focus and concentration for the next term.
